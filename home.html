History of U.S. Salish Sea hydrophone networks

Chronology of hydrophones in the Salish Sea (U.S. and Canada, including Johnstone Strait)
  • '70s: Single hydrophones: Rich Osborne, Research Director of The Whale Museum, initiates SeaSound Remote Sensing Network; intermittent deployments by Ken Balcomb (Center for Whale Research)
  • 80s-90s: Hydrophone localization array deployed at Lime Kiln by Rich, David Bain, and Joe Olson (Cetacean Research Technologies); Vashon Hydrophone Project of the American Cetacean Society runs for ~5 years
  • ~2003: Val and Colorado College students establish Orcasound Lab between Lime Kiln and Roche Harbor
  • ~2005-2012: Beam Reach students use and help maintain hydrophones at Lime Kiln (Jason Wood, then Beam Reach faculty and/or Research Director at TWM) and Orcasound Lab, with funding assistance from WDFW, Beam Reach, and Cornell University
  • 2007-2012: Funding from NOAA to expand from Lime Kiln to 5 locations; TWM grant with Val and Scott of Beam Reach as co-PIs.
  • 2012-2015: Beam Reach maintains network
  • 2016-18: Ten+ network members reorganize/re-brand as Orcasound; Jason (now with SMRU Consulting) maintains Lime Kiln with TWM
  • 2017: Beam Reach runs $20k Kickstarter campaign to re-engineer Orcasound hardware and software
  • 2018: Beta-testing and public release of new Orcasound infrastructure
